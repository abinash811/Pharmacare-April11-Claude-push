Voice & Tone
Professional, functional, direct. Never playful.
PharmaCare is a daily operational tool for pharmacy staff. Copy should be as short as possible. Users are completing tasks, not reading. Every word must earn its place.

Tone: Confident, calm, precise. Like a well-trained pharmacist — not a chatbot.
Not: Cheerful, trendy, emoji-heavy, or vague. Never "Oops!" or "Yikes!" or "Awesome!"
India context: Rupee (₹), Indian names, GST, Schedule H — assume Indian pharmacy workflow.

What We Never Use
No emoji anywhere in the interface (status indicators use colored badges, not emoji).
No exclamation marks in UI copy — "Bill saved!" → "Bill saved".
No "Please" in form labels or error messages — "Please enter email" → "Enter email".
No passive voice — "Bill was saved" → "Bill saved".
No abbreviations without context — "Qty" is fine (standard pharmacy term); "Inv" alone is not.
No lorem ipsum anywhere in shipping UI — use realistic pharmacy data in demos.
No ellipsis in buttons — buttons say what they do, not "More...".
"Walk-in" not "Walk In" or "Walkin" — consistent hyphenation for counter sales.
